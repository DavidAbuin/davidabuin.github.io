Summary

David Abuín Eichinger is a Master's student in the Department of Computer Science at George Mason University. He received his Post-baccalaureate Certificate in Computer Science from GMU. He also received his B.S. degrees in Microbiology from North Carolina State University and Maritime Transportation from the United States Merchant Marine Academy. He is a US Navy veteran with joint operational planning and knowledge management experience. David's interests include biomimicry and bio-inspired robotics with the aim of using robotics in various biological applications such as laboratory automation, environmental restoration, agriculture, and providing autonomous assistance for caretakers of senior citizens and those with disabilities.

In his free time, he enjoys reading, playing with family, gardening, and physical fitness activities such as weightlifting, running, cycling, and hiking. He had recently completed the Army 10 miler in Washington, DC and the Mason 5K.

US Naval Officer

Research

Research Experience

David Eichinger is currently a member of the Robotixx Lab at George Mason University under the supervision of Dr. Xuesu Xiao. The primary mission of the lab is to develop highly capable and intelligent mobile robots that are robustly deployable in the real world with minimal human supervision.

His previous research experience was as a research assistant in the cell biology lab of Dr. Chad Pearon of the University of Colorado Anschutz. The primary focus of the lab was to understand centriole assembly using multiple mammalian cell lines and the protozoan Tetrahymena thermophila as a model organism. David managed laboratory equipment and supplies as well as conducted various experiments with the Tetrahymena to elucidate understanding of centriole structure and assembly.
